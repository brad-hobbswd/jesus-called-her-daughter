Part Four • The Greater Story

Chapter Eleven

Twelve Years and Four Days

Throughout the Gospels, Jesus performed miracles that revealed not only His compassion but also His divine authority. Each miracle addressed a different human need, yet together they proclaimed one unmistakable truth. Nothing is impossible for the Son of God.

The healing of the woman with the issue of blood and the raising of Lazarus appear to tell two very different stories. One woman suffered from a disease that slowly consumed her strength over twelve long years. One man died, was buried, and remained in a tomb for four days. One miracle restored a life that was fading. The other restored a life that had already ended.

Yet these accounts are united by a common message. Jesus Christ possesses absolute authority over both sickness and death.

The woman endured twelve years of continual suffering. Every day was marked by weakness, disappointment, and unanswered questions. She searched for physicians, spent everything she owned, and exhausted every earthly solution. By the time she reached Jesus, hope seemed impossible according to human understanding.

Lazarus experienced a different trial. His sickness ended not in recovery but in death. His sisters, Martha and Mary, watched the one they loved breathe his last breath. Family members prepared his body for burial. Friends gathered to mourn. Four days passed as grief settled over the household in Bethany.

From every human perspective, both situations appeared beyond hope.

One body could not stop bleeding.

The other no longer breathed.

One family watched life slowly disappear.

The other believed life had ended forever.

Yet both stories remind us that God's timing is often different from our own.

The woman wondered why healing had not come sooner. Martha and Mary wondered why Jesus had not arrived before Lazarus died. In both accounts, people struggled to understand why the Lord appeared to delay.

How often have believers asked the same question?

Why did God wait?

Why was healing delayed?

Why did the answer seem to come so late?

Scripture does not always explain God's timing, but it consistently reveals His faithfulness. Delays are never evidence that God has forgotten His people. More often, they become opportunities for His glory to be displayed in ways we could never imagine.

The woman needed healing.

Lazarus needed resurrection.

Jesus provided both.

When Christ arrived at the tomb of Lazarus, the situation appeared irreversible. Martha herself said, "Lord, by this time there will be an odor, for he has been dead four days." Human ability had reached its absolute limit. Physicians could do nothing. Family could do nothing. Friends could do nothing.

Jesus stood before the tomb and spoke only three words.

"Lazarus, come forth."

The man who had been dead walked out alive.

The same authority that stopped twelve years of continual bleeding also called a dead man from his grave. Disease obeyed Him. Death obeyed Him. Creation itself obeyed Him because He is Lord over all.

These miracles remind us that no circumstance is beyond the reach of Christ. Some people live with burdens that develop slowly over many years. Others encounter sudden tragedy that changes everything overnight. Whether suffering lasts twelve years or four days, the Savior remains the same.

He is not limited by time.

He is not overwhelmed by impossible circumstances.

He is not surprised by human suffering.

His authority has no boundaries.

For the woman with the issue of blood, healing came through a touch of faith. For Lazarus, life returned through the commanding voice of the Son of God. In both accounts, the miracle rested entirely upon Christ's power rather than human effort.

These stories also remind us that Jesus cares deeply about individuals. He stopped for one forgotten woman while walking to another urgent need. He wept beside the tomb of His friend before demonstrating His authority over death. His miracles were never displays of power for their own sake. They were expressions of divine love flowing from the heart of God.

Many people today continue to carry burdens that seem impossible. Some battle chronic illness. Others grieve the loss of someone they love. Some pray for answers that appear delayed, while others struggle to believe that God still sees their pain.

The stories of the woman and Lazarus speak hope into every one of those situations.

If Christ possesses authority over disease, there is hope.

If Christ possesses authority over death, there is hope.

If Christ conquered the grave through His own resurrection, there is eternal hope for every believer.

The greatest miracle of all is not simply that Jesus healed the sick or raised the dead.

The greatest miracle is that through His death, burial, and resurrection He conquered sin itself, opening the way for all who believe to receive everlasting life.

The woman's healing pointed toward that greater victory.

Lazarus's resurrection pointed toward that greater victory.

Every miracle in the Gospels ultimately points to Jesus Christ Himself.

He is the Great Physician.

He is the Resurrection and the Life.

He is the Savior of the world.

Whether our need is healing for today or hope for eternity, every answer is found in Him.

The woman's story reminds us that Christ has authority over sickness.

Lazarus's story reminds us that Christ has authority over death.

Together they proclaim the same glorious truth.

Jesus Christ is Lord over all.
