When Jesus Called Her Daughter

Epilogue

Daughter

Every story eventually reaches its final page, but the work of Jesus Christ never does. The woman with the issue of blood disappeared from the pages of Scripture as quietly as she entered them. The Gospels never tell us where she lived afterward, how many years she enjoyed her restored health, or whether she ever saw Jesus again before His crucifixion and resurrection. Her earthly story fades from view, but the truth revealed through her life continues to speak across the centuries.

Perhaps that is exactly as God intended.

Her story was never meant to end with her.

It was meant to point every generation to Jesus.

When we first met this unnamed woman, she was weary, isolated, and desperate. Twelve years of suffering had changed every part of her life. Physicians could not help her. Society misunderstood her. Her resources were exhausted, and hope seemed nearly gone. Yet none of those circumstances defined her future because they did not have the final word.

Jesus did.

The same is true for every believer today.

Many people carry burdens that cannot be seen. Some struggle with chronic illness. Others battle anxiety, depression, grief, addiction, loneliness, or deep wounds left by betrayal and rejection. Many quietly ask the same questions this woman may have asked.

Will life ever change?

Does God see me?

Have I been forgotten?

Is there still hope?

The Gospel answers every one of those questions with the person of Jesus Christ.

He still sees those whom others overlook.

He still hears the prayers whispered through tears.

He still welcomes those who feel unworthy to come.

He still restores what sin, suffering, and sorrow have broken.

The greatest miracle recorded in this story was never simply the healing of a physical disease. Physical healing, wonderful as it is, remains temporary. Every person whom Jesus healed during His earthly ministry eventually faced death. Even Lazarus, whom Jesus called from the tomb, would one day die again.

There is, however, a greater miracle.

Through His death upon the cross, His burial, and His glorious resurrection, Jesus accomplished what no physician, priest, or prophet could ever accomplish. He made a way for sinful men and women to be reconciled to God. He bore our sins so that we might receive His righteousness. He conquered death so that everyone who believes in Him might receive everlasting life.

This is the heart of the Gospel.

The woman came seeking healing.

She found the Healer.

Every person who comes to Christ seeking mercy discovers something even greater than the answer they expected. We discover the Savior Himself.

That is why the word Jesus spoke remains so powerful.

"Daughter."

He did not define her by twelve years of suffering.

He did not call her according to her weakness.

He did not identify her by the labels others had placed upon her.

He called her according to relationship.

That same invitation continues today.

Through faith in Jesus Christ, those who were once separated from God are welcomed into His family. The Scriptures describe believers as children of God, heirs with Christ, and members of His household. What Jesus declared over one faithful woman reflects the heart of the Gospel for every believer.

Grace gives us a new identity.

Our past no longer defines us.

Our failures no longer own us.

Our wounds no longer determine our future.

In Christ, we become new creations.

As you close this book, perhaps you see yourself somewhere within her story. Maybe you identify with her waiting, her questions, or her longing for restoration. Perhaps you have carried burdens that few people understand. Maybe you have wondered whether God still sees your tears or hears your prayers.

If so, remember the lesson that echoes through every chapter of her journey.

Jesus never overlooked her.

He has not overlooked you.

He knew her suffering before she reached through the crowd.

He knew her faith before she touched His garment.

He knew her name before He called her "Daughter."

He knows yours as well.

The woman with the issue of blood reminds us that no life is beyond the reach of God's grace. No wound is too deep. No failure is too great. No heart is too broken for the mercy of Christ.

The invitation remains exactly the same today as it was on that crowded street so many centuries ago.

Come to Jesus.

Come with your questions.

Come with your fears.

Come with your failures.

Come with your brokenness.

Come in faith.

You may come seeking healing.

You may come seeking hope.

You may come searching for answers.

But above all else, you will find the Savior.

And there is no greater miracle than belonging to Him.

May the Lord bless you, strengthen your faith, and remind you every day that His grace is greater than your deepest sorrow and His love reaches farther than your greatest need.

May you always remember the woman who reached through the crowd.

More importantly, may you always remember the Savior who stopped for her.

Because He is still stopping for people today.

And by His grace, He is still calling sons and daughters home.
