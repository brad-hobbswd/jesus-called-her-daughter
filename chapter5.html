Part Two • Hope Begins to Rise

Chapter Five

Faith Comes by Hearing

For twelve years the woman with the issue of blood had searched for healing through every means available to her. Physicians had failed. Remedies had failed. Her finances had disappeared, and her body continued to weaken. Yet even after years of disappointment, one thing remained alive within her heart. She had not completely lost hope.

Hope often arrives in unexpected ways. Sometimes it comes through the quiet encouragement of a friend. Sometimes it is found while reading the Scriptures. At other times, hope arrives through a simple report that changes everything we believe is possible.

For this unnamed woman, hope came through the news spreading across Galilee about a man called Jesus of Nazareth.

The Gospel of Mark tells us, "She had heard the reports concerning Jesus." Those few words reveal the beginning of one of the greatest demonstrations of faith found in the New Testament. Before she ever saw Him, she heard about Him. Before she reached through the crowd, His reputation had already reached her heart.

The ministry of Jesus spread quickly throughout the region. Villages buzzed with conversations about the Rabbi who taught with authority unlike the scribes. Travelers carried stories from one town to another. Fishermen spoke about Him along the shores of the Sea of Galilee. Merchants repeated accounts they had heard in the marketplaces. Families gathered around evening meals discussing the miracles they had witnessed or heard described by others.

Some spoke about the leper who had been cleansed with a single touch. Others told of blind men receiving their sight, deaf ears being opened, crippled people walking again, and storms obeying His voice. Parents rejoiced because their children had been healed. Entire communities witnessed lives transformed in ways no one could explain apart from the power of God.

Every story carried another testimony.

Every testimony carried another invitation.

Every invitation whispered the same possibility.

"Perhaps He can help you too."

We are not told who first spoke to the woman about Jesus. It may have been a neighbor returning from another village. Perhaps a family member repeated what they had heard from travelers. Maybe someone who had personally witnessed one of Christ's miracles shared the story with her. Scripture remains silent, allowing us to focus not on the messenger but on the message itself.

She heard about Jesus.

That hearing changed everything.

Years later, the Apostle Paul would write that "faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God." Although those words were written after this miracle took place, they beautifully describe what happened within this woman's heart. Faith was born before she ever took her first step toward Christ. It began the moment she believed what she had heard.

This is often how God works. Long before our circumstances change, He begins changing our hearts. Before prayers are answered, He plants hope. Before miracles become visible, He strengthens faith where no one else can see it. The answer may still be on the horizon, but confidence in God quietly begins to grow.

Imagine what she must have felt as she listened to one account after another. Every miracle challenged the conclusions she had accepted after twelve years of suffering. If Jesus could cleanse a leper, perhaps He could heal continual bleeding. If He could restore sight to the blind, perhaps He could restore strength to her weary body. If demons fled at His command and storms obeyed His voice, then surely no sickness was beyond His authority.

Little by little, despair surrendered to expectation.

Discouragement gave way to anticipation.

Hope began replacing fear.

Somewhere deep within her heart, a conviction formed that could no longer be shaken. She no longer believed healing was impossible. She believed healing had a name.

Faith, however, is more than believing something is possible. Biblical faith responds. It moves. It acts upon what it believes.

The woman could have listened to the stories about Jesus and remained where she was. She could have admired His miracles from a distance while convincing herself that they were meant for someone else. Instead, the reports she heard awakened a faith that refused to remain passive.

She made a decision.

If Jesus truly possessed the authority everyone described, she would go to Him.

She would not wait for Him to seek her out.

She would seek Him.

Mark records the words she quietly settled within her own heart: "If I but touch His clothes, I shall be made well."

Notice what she did not say.

She did not say, "If another physician discovers the answer."

She did not say, "If my condition improves on its own."

She did not say, "If someone notices me."

Her confidence rested entirely upon Jesus.

This confidence was not rooted in superstition. She was not placing her trust in a piece of cloth or in some mystical object. Her faith rested in the One wearing the garment. She believed that the authority of God was present in Jesus Christ and that even the slightest contact with Him would accomplish what twelve years of human effort never could.

The reports she had heard were no longer merely stories.

They had become personal conviction.

Conviction was about to become action.

The road ahead would not be easy. A crowd separated her from Christ. Weakness still burdened her body. Fear still whispered that she might be rejected or exposed. Yet faith had already won the battle within her heart.

The next step was simply to begin walking.

Every miracle has a moment that comes before it, when faith chooses to believe despite every visible reason to doubt. This was her moment. She had heard enough to know that Jesus was different from every physician she had ever visited. Human wisdom had reached its limits, but the mercy of God was only beginning to unfold.

Hope had found a voice.

Faith had found its direction.

Now she would begin the journey that would lead her into the presence of the Savior.
