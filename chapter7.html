Part Three • One Touch Changed Everything

Chapter Seven

The Touch of Faith

For twelve years the woman with the issue of blood had searched for healing everywhere except the place where it would ultimately be found. She had visited physicians, endured painful treatments, and spent every possession she owned. Each new remedy promised relief, yet every attempt ended with the same disappointing result. Instead of becoming better, she grew worse. By every human measure, her situation appeared hopeless.

Then she heard about Jesus.

Everything changed.

Her healing did not begin when she touched His garment. It began when faith entered her heart. Before she reached with her hand, she first believed with her heart. Every step through the crowd was evidence of an inward confidence that Jesus possessed what no one else could give.

Mark records her words with remarkable simplicity: "If I but touch His clothes, I shall be made well." Those words reveal far more than optimism. They reveal conviction. She believed that the authority of God rested upon Jesus Christ. She believed that His presence carried power greater than twelve years of sickness.

Many readers have wondered why she reached for His garment instead of simply asking Him for help. Scripture does not tell us exactly why, but several possibilities help us appreciate her faith.

Perhaps she feared drawing attention to herself because of her ceremonial uncleanness. She may have hoped to receive healing quietly without interrupting Jesus or embarrassing herself before the crowd.

Perhaps she believed herself unworthy to stand before Him publicly. After twelve years of rejection and isolation, she may have struggled to imagine that the Rabbi everyone admired would stop for someone like her.

Or perhaps her faith had become so complete that she believed she needed nothing more than the slightest contact with the Savior.

Whatever her reasoning, one truth remains certain.

Her confidence rested entirely upon Jesus.

The garment itself possessed no miraculous power. The fabric was ordinary. The tassels attached to His robe were reminders of God's covenant with Israel, but they did not heal anyone by themselves. The miracle came from Christ, not from His clothing.

This distinction is important because biblical faith never places its confidence in objects. Faith rests in the living God. Throughout history people have often been tempted to place their trust in religious symbols, sacred places, or treasured possessions. While such things may remind us of God's presence, they possess no power apart from Him.

The woman understood, perhaps more clearly than many in the crowd, that Jesus Himself was the source of healing.

As she stretched out her trembling hand, years of disappointment met unwavering faith. The distance between despair and hope became no greater than the reach of her fingertips.

Then it happened.

Immediately the flow of blood stopped.

Mark tells us that she felt within her body that she had been healed of her affliction. There was no waiting period. No gradual recovery. No uncertainty about whether the miracle had taken place.

The healing was immediate.

Complete.

Undeniable.

For twelve years her body had steadily weakened. In a single moment it was restored.

At the very same instant, Jesus perceived that power had gone out from Him. This does not suggest that Christ lost strength or became weaker. Rather, it demonstrates His divine awareness of what had just taken place. The miracle was intentional. The healing was personal. Nothing escaped His knowledge.

Here we discover one of the most beautiful truths in the entire account.

Many people touched Jesus that day.

Only one person touched Him in faith.

The disciples saw a crowd pressing against their Master from every direction. To them, one touch seemed no different from another. Yet Jesus recognized the difference immediately.

There is a profound difference between being near Jesus and trusting Him.

Thousands heard His teaching.

Many witnessed His miracles.

Crowds surrounded Him wherever He traveled.

Yet not everyone believed.

It is possible to admire Jesus without surrendering to Him. It is possible to attend worship services, read Scripture, and know facts about Christ while never placing complete trust in Him. The crowds experienced His presence, but this woman experienced His power because her faith rested entirely in Him.

Her touch was small.

Her faith was great.

Not because she believed in herself.

Not because she possessed extraordinary courage.

But because her confidence was placed in the One who is able to do exceedingly abundantly above all that we ask or think.

The miracle teaches us that genuine faith is never measured by the size of the action but by the object of that faith. A trembling hand reaching toward Christ is stronger than the greatest confidence placed anywhere else.

For twelve years she had reached toward physicians.

Now she reached toward Jesus.

Everything changed.

Yet the greatest miracle was still unfolding.

The bleeding had stopped.

Her body had been restored.

But Jesus had not finished speaking.

The Savior who healed her in private was about to honor her in public, and the next words He spoke would forever transform her identity.
