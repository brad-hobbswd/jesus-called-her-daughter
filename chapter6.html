Part Two • Hope Begins to Rise

Chapter Six

Through the Crowd

Faith had awakened within her heart, but faith alone did not remove the obstacles standing between her and Jesus. The reports she had heard filled her with hope, yet hope still required a response. There would be no healing unless she acted upon what she believed.

For twelve years she had lived behind the boundaries created by sickness, weakness, and ceremonial uncleanness. Every day had reminded her of what she could not do. Every disappointment had tempted her to remain where she was. Yet something within her had changed. The woman who had once searched for physicians was now searching for Christ.

Scripture does not tell us how far she traveled to find Jesus. Whether she walked only a few streets or journeyed from another village, every step demanded determination. Her body was still weak. Her condition had not improved. The miracle had not yet happened.

Faith often begins walking before circumstances begin changing.

It is easy to believe after the miracle.

It is far more difficult to believe before it.

As she approached the place where Jesus was teaching, she discovered what many others had already discovered. He was never alone for very long. Wherever He traveled, crowds gathered around Him. Some came seeking healing. Others came out of curiosity. Religious leaders often came looking for reasons to challenge Him, while ordinary people simply longed to hear His words.

The crowd represented more than people.

It represented obstacles.

Every person standing between her and Jesus became another reminder of the risks she faced. According to the ceremonial customs of her day, she knew others would have considered her unclean. If recognized, she could have been publicly embarrassed, rebuked, or turned away before she ever reached Christ.

Fear would have been understandable.

Shame would have been understandable.

Turning around would have been understandable.

Yet faith continued moving forward.

How many times do we allow fear to stop us before we ever discover what God intends to do? We fear rejection. We fear failure. We fear disappointment. The woman with the issue of blood certainly had reasons to fear, but she refused to allow fear to become the author of her story.

She pressed forward.

The crowd grew thicker.

Voices surrounded her from every direction. People stretched to catch a glimpse of Jesus. Some called out His name. Others brought sick family members hoping for healing. Children weaved between adults while the disciples attempted to make room for their Master to continue walking.

To everyone else, she was simply another face in the crowd.

To Jesus, she was already known.

The Gospel writers tell us that she said within herself, "If I only touch His garments, I shall be restored to health." These were not words spoken aloud. They were the quiet confession of a heart that had placed all its hope in Christ.

Notice what she did not say.

She did not say, "If another physician discovers the answer."

She did not say, "If my condition improves on its own."

She did not say, "If someone notices me."

Her confidence rested entirely upon Jesus.

Jewish men commonly wore outer garments with tassels attached to the corners in obedience to the commandments given in the Law. These tassels served as reminders of God's covenant and His commandments. Whether she intended to touch one of those tassels or simply the edge of His garment, her purpose remained the same. She believed that contact with Jesus would accomplish what twelve years of suffering never could.

This was not magic.

It was not superstition.

It was faith reaching toward the One in whom all authority dwelled.

Step by step the distance between her and Jesus disappeared.

One more step.

Then another.

The sounds of the crowd faded beneath the determination growing within her heart. She was no longer thinking about those around her. She was thinking only about reaching the Savior.

Finally, she was close enough.

She stretched out her trembling hand.

For one brief moment, twelve years of pain, loneliness, disappointment, unanswered prayers, fading strength, and exhausted resources came down to a single act of faith.

Her fingers touched the edge of His garment.

Immediately the bleeding stopped.

The weakness that had plagued her for twelve years disappeared.

Life flowed back into a body that had been slowly drained day after day. She knew instantly that something extraordinary had happened. This was not gradual improvement. This was complete healing.

Mark records that she immediately felt within herself that she had been healed of her affliction. There was no uncertainty. No waiting for symptoms to fade. No wondering if the treatment had worked. In a single moment, what no physician could accomplish in twelve years, Jesus accomplished without speaking a word.

Yet the miracle was not finished.

At that same moment, Jesus stopped walking.

Surrounded by hundreds of people pressing against Him from every side, He turned and asked a question that surprised everyone around Him.

"Who touched My clothes?"

The disciples could hardly believe what they were hearing. They looked at the crowd surrounding Jesus and replied that people were pressing against Him from every direction. How could He ask who had touched Him when dozens of people were brushing against Him every moment?

But Jesus was not speaking about an accidental touch.

He was speaking about a touch of faith.

Many hands touched Him that day.

Only one heart reached Him.

The woman who had spent twelve years trying not to be noticed suddenly realized that the Son of God had noticed her all along. The miracle had restored her body, but Jesus was preparing to restore something even greater.

He was about to restore her identity.

The next words He spoke would not simply confirm her healing. They would forever change the way generations of believers remembered her story.
