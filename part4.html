When Jesus Called Her Daughter

Part Four

The Greater Story

The miracle did not end when the bleeding stopped, nor did its significance end when Jesus called the woman "Daughter." Like every miracle recorded in Scripture, her healing pointed beyond itself to a greater truth. It revealed the character of Christ, the purpose of redemption, and the hope that belongs to every believer who places their faith in Him.

The woman with the issue of blood was more than the recipient of a miracle. She became a living testimony that Jesus possesses authority over everything that separates humanity from God. Her story reminds us that Christ does not merely remove sickness. He restores lives, renews hope, and welcomes broken people into His family.

This final section broadens our view from one miraculous encounter to the greater story of redemption. It reveals that the same Savior who healed one forgotten woman continues to restore hearts, conquer death, and offer eternal hope to all who come to Him in faith.

Her story became our story.

Her Savior is our Savior.

Chapter Ten

Holiness That Heals

According to ceremonial expectations, uncleanness spread through contact. Yet when the woman touched Jesus, the opposite occurred. His holiness overcame her uncleanness. This chapter explores how Christ fulfilled the Law, revealed the true nature of holiness, and gave a beautiful picture of redemption that still speaks today.

Chapter Eleven

Twelve Years and Four Days

The woman with the issue of blood suffered for twelve years. Lazarus lay in the tomb for four days. One story reveals Christ's authority over chronic suffering. The other reveals His authority over death itself. Together they proclaim that nothing is impossible for the Son of God.

Chapter Twelve

Remembered Forever

Although Scripture never records her name, her faith has been remembered for nearly two thousand years. This chapter considers the early Christian tradition surrounding the bronze statue believed by some to honor her healing, while showing that her greatest memorial is not found in bronze, but in the eternal Word of God and the legacy of faith she left for every generation.

Epilogue

Daughter

The woman's story ultimately points beyond physical healing to the greater miracle of salvation. Jesus still calls broken people into His family, replacing shame with grace, fear with peace, and separation with belonging. The same Savior who spoke the word "Daughter" continues to invite every repentant heart to experience restoration through Him.
